# World Facts

This file contains continuity-critical facts about the Transcendence setting.

Its role is narrow:

- record stable world facts that prose must not contradict
- give agents a compact continuity anchor before writing or checking lore-facing text
- separate established facts from open questions, interpretation, and presentation choices

If a fact is not stable enough to constrain prose yet, it does not belong here.

---

## Scope rule

This file is not:

- a full setting bible
- a timeline
- a mythology encyclopedia
- a dump of possible ideas

This file is:

- a continuity lock for facts that already constrain writing

---

## Naming and identity

- The setting / universe name is **Transcendence**.
- The in-world parallel dimension relevant to extranatural phenomena is **the Limbo**.
- **Tauma** is the energy originating in the Limbo.
- **the Void** is not the same thing as the Limbo.

---

## Ontological facts

- The Limbo exists in parallel to the physical world.
- Normal creatures do not perceive the Limbo directly.
- The Limbo does not interact with the physical world as direct matter-to-matter contact; its influence is known through energy filtration and manifestation.
- Tauma does not create phenomena from nothing. It transforms, modifies, or amplifies what already exists in the material world.

---

## Extranatural facts

- Extranatural phenomena are defined by origin, not by intensity.
- A phenomenon is extranatural when it results from Limbo energy filtering into the perceptible world.
- Observable contradictions are signs of Limbo presence, not the full definition of the phenomenon.
- A phenomenon may be extranatural even if it does not display spectacular or visibly contradictory effects.
- Extranatural conditions belong to the perceptible world; they are not the Limbo itself, but effects of its influence on material reality.

---

## Limbo manifestation facts

The Limbo reaches the world through three recognized manifestation types:

- **ambient flow**: passive diffuse filtration of Limbo energy in an area
- **vestige**: residual Tauma left in an object or place after prior contact
- **link**: active bridge between the physical world and the Limbo

Stable distinctions:

- A vestige is not a link.
- A vestige has no active ongoing connection to the Limbo.
- A link involves continuous energy flow and is more dangerous than a vestige.
- Ambient flow is environmental rather than object-anchored.

---

## Theology and interpretation

- In the elemental religion, Limbo-related phenomena are attributed to **Kaodis**, deity of the Void.
- Kaodis is a theological interpretation of these phenomena, not the mechanical definition of their cause.
- Theological interpretation and mechanical explanation must not be treated as interchangeable unless a text is explicitly speaking from an in-world doctrinal viewpoint.

---

## Perception constraints

- Normal creatures cannot perceive the Limbo directly.
- Perception of Limbo-related influence depends on its effects, markers, or specialized means of detection.
- A text should not describe direct Limbo perception from an ordinary viewpoint unless the character or creature has an established reason to perceive it.

---

## Combat and world-state continuity

- Changes to encounter reality can become facts about the world-state of the scene.
- A world-state change persists independently of whether the character who caused it remains present, conscious, or alive.
- Continuity should track what changed in reality, not only what characters currently know.

---

## Publication constraints derived from these facts

- Do not describe the Limbo as a visible everyday layer accessible to ordinary perception.
- Do not describe Tauma as spontaneous creation ex nihilo.
- Do not use “the Void” and “the Limbo” as synonyms.
- Do not present theological explanation as mechanical certainty in neutral narration.
- Do not describe every extranatural event as loud, explosive, or visibly contradictory; subtle extranatural change is valid.

---

## Continuity review triggers

When reviewing a prose file, stop and check `world-facts.md` immediately if the text does any of the following:

- describes direct perception of the Limbo
- explains the origin of an extranatural event
- names the Void and the Limbo in the same passage
- uses theology to explain a phenomenon in neutral narration
- describes Tauma as generating matter, force, or effects from nothing
- treats subtle extranatural events as if visible contradiction were required

If one of these appears, the reviewer must confirm the passage is consistent with the facts above before the file can advance.

---

## Continuity questions reviewers should ask

Use these questions during continuity review:

1. Is the passage describing a world fact, a character belief, or a theological interpretation?
2. If the passage mentions the Limbo, could the viewpoint actually perceive what is being described?
3. If the passage mentions Tauma, is it transforming what exists or incorrectly creating from nothing?
4. If the passage mentions the Void, is it being used as theology rather than as a synonym for the Limbo?
5. If the passage describes an extranatural event, is the text distinguishing origin from intensity?

If the answer is unclear, the passage is not ready for `final`.

---

## Facts intentionally excluded from this file

The following do **not** belong here until they are settled as stable canon:

- unresolved cosmology
- detailed religious doctrine beyond continuity-critical distinctions
- unresolved Tauma system rules
- unresolved species, faction, or geography details
- design questions currently tracked as open in `Transcendence-design/docs/pending.md`

When one of those becomes stable enough to constrain prose globally, promote it into this file.
