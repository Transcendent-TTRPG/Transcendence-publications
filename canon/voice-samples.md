# Voice Samples

**Status: skeleton — needs approved prose samples from project owner.**

This file is the rhythm reference for all Transcendence prose. Samples here are treated as the gold standard. When rewriting a passage, find a sample with similar purpose and use it as a template for cadence and vocabulary — not content.

---

## How to use this file

1. Identify the purpose of the passage you are writing (rules explanation, scene-setting, example, etc.).
2. Find a sample below with the same purpose.
3. Read the sample aloud to internalize the rhythm.
4. Write your passage. Match the sentence length pattern. Match the level of technical density. Do not copy phrases.

## What this file needs (instructions for project owner)

Please provide 2–4 paragraphs for each of the following categories. These should be paragraphs you consider already written well — passages you would be proud to show as representative of the project's voice.

### Required sample categories

**A. Rules explanation (ES)** — A paragraph that explains a mechanical concept clearly and without hedging. Ideally from the combat or attrition chapters.

**B. Rules explanation (EN)** — Same as above but in English.

**C. Scene or narrative (ES)** — A paragraph with more literary weight. Could be an in-world example, a piece of flavor text, or a light novel excerpt.

**D. Scene or narrative (EN)** — Same as above but in English.

**E. Example of play (ES or EN)** — A passage that walks through a specific in-game situation. Shows how the rules feel in practice without being dry.
