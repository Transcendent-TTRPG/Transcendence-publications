# Poisons

**Poisons** are toxic substances that, upon contact with an organism, produce adverse effects. They may be natural or artificial in origin and come in many forms — liquids, gases, biological extracts from creatures — with a range from mild discomfort to severe consequences.

Poisons differ from Alterations in that they are not mechanical disruptions of the body caused by external forces: they are agents that enter the organism through a delivery method and act from within.

**Resistance Roll:** `1d10 + Tenacity + Poison Resistance level + additional bonuses`

---

## Poison Kit

Handling any poison requires a **Poison Kit** appropriate to the poison's rarity. Without the corresponding kit, the risk of self-contamination is high:

| Poison severity | Self-contamination risk |
| --- | --- |
| Minor | 50% |
| Moderate | 75% |
| Severe | 100% |

---

## Identification

Characters with knowledge in **Understanding (Alchemy)** can identify unknown poisons. The complexity of the identification is determined by the poison's **Alchemical Index**:

| Alchemical Index | Level | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2–4 | Fundamentals | Basic poisons; minor risks and effects |
| 5–6 | Challenging | Intermediate complexity; moderate risks and effects |
| 7–8 | Rigorous | Complex poisons; significant effects and risks |
| 9 | Demanding | Very complex poisons; dangerous effects |
| 10 | Extreme | Exceptional potency and danger |

---

## Application

| Context | Time |
| --- | --- |
| Outside combat | ~1 minute |
| In combat | 1 standard action |

Poisons may be applied to weapons, food, or directly to the target, depending on the poison's nature and delivery method.

---

## Delivery Methods

| Method | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Inoculation** | Direct introduction into the bloodstream — bites, stings, impregnated weapons |
| **Ingestion** | The poison enters through the digestive system — food, drinks, contaminated plants |
| **Inhalation** | The poison is absorbed through the lungs — gases, vapors, powders |
| **Contact** | The poison is absorbed through the skin — requires direct exposure or compromised skin |

---

## Duration

Poisons have `until_removed` duration. They persist in the organism until actively neutralized.

**Neutralization:** Stopping an active poison's effect in an organism can be achieved through specific antidotes, healing techniques, or alchemical methods. Difficulty depends on the poison's potency and nature.
