# Infections

**Infections** are biological or contaminant agents that infiltrate the organism, persist within it, and may spread to other creatures. Unlike Poisons — which act once and are static — Infections are dynamic: they have an incubation period, can worsen over time without treatment, and are capable of spreading through contact.

**Resistance Roll:** `1d10 + Tenacity + Infection Resistance level + additional bonuses`

---

## Contagion

**Contagion** represents the difficulty of contracting an Infection after initial exposure. When a character is exposed to an infectious source, they must pass an **Infections R.R.** at the difficulty specified in that Infection's entry.

If they fail, the Infection establishes itself in their organism and the incubation period begins.

---

## Incubation

Each Infection has a variable **incubation period** depending on the disease. During this period the Infection is present in the organism but asymptomatic — there are no visible negative effects on the character.

Once the incubation period ends, symptoms manifest, the Infection's effects activate, and the Infection may begin to spread.

---

## Propagation

Once an Infection has passed its incubation period and is active, it can spread to other creatures through **direct physical contact**. Any creature that makes physical contact with an active Infection carrier must pass an **Infections R.R.** at the difficulty specified in that Infection's entry to avoid being infected.

The contagion difficulty and the propagation difficulty may differ — each Infection entry specifies both if they are not the same.
